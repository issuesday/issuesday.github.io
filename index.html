About Issues Day

Welcome to Issues Day!

It is an annual Saint Mary’s Hall tradition to conduct an in-depth discussion of an intellectual and timely topic. Allowing open and direct study of difficult subjects provides a wonderful compliment to the students and faculty of our school. To encourage such dialogue on the toughest questions of our time shows respect for our students’ intellect and maturity while highlighting our faculty’s professionalism and abilities in working with our students to help them develop their own beliefs and opinions regarding difficult topics.

As in the past, this year’s topic is not one which yields an easy answer or simple consensus. However, our students’ active participation will help them view opposing opinions as they solidify their own thoughts and beliefs. We are encouraging our community to examine the topic with openness and respect for self and others.

I want to thank the Junior Class Issues Day Committee. The speaker line-up alone serves as excellent evidence of the Committee’s success and dedication. I also want to express particular and deep gratitude to the faculty leadership on the Issues Day Committee: Barb Bertoldo, Jen- nifer Arce, Gerri Walls, Jeff Hebert, and Megan Soukup. Enjoy the discourse!
